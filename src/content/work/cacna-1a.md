A collaborative art project between father and son exploring gene mutation, processing a life altered, and the unexpected blessings of disability.

During a hospital visit with my son, I watched him create art with no consequence — no audience, no goal, just making. I had become despondent that my professional practice was beginning to feel stale, and seeking a new outlet, I turned to 3D. It was a way to connect with my son and his disability on his terms.

Taking his naive sketches and approach, I turned them into splines and explored dynamic texturing and lighting in Redshift — a process I hadn't tried before. The result: my own naive abstractions. We made art together and explored NFTs in the process.
